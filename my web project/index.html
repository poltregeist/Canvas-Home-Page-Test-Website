What is a learning organization?

A learning organization is a values-driven organization with systems, conventions, practices, and processes to achieve organizational goals. This organization is innovative and able to deal with change by building organizational capacity through personal mastery and team learning.

Peter Senge (Harvard Professor) listed 5 practices of a learning organization:

  • Personal Mastery: Continually clarifying and deepening our personal vision.
  • Mental Models: Deeply ingrained assumptions, images, and generalizations that influence how we act. Ability to reflect in and on actions.
  • Shared Vision: Building a shared vision and sense of collective commitment as to where we want to go as an organization and how to achieve that goal. With vision, people excel and learn not because they are told to but because they want to.
  • Team Learning: Process of aligning and developing team capacities to create results they truly desire to see.
  • Systems Thinking: Comprehend and address the whole and examine interrelationships between parts.

What does ASI as a learning organization look and feel like?

  • We feel safe to continually challenge ourselves and each other to do better and be better versions of ourselves.
  • We use a framework to make decisions that are directly tied back to our core values.
  • We design better ways of doing our “ever-changing work” with strategies that take into account everyone that is affected and create a continuous improvement mindset and, in turn, shared ownership for projects and shared accountability for results.

Here are some of the core behaviors we’ll learn and practice during this course:

  • Exhibiting a growth mindset
  • Being coachable and vulnerable
  • Practicing radical candor
  • Having an abundance of care
  • Creating space for self-efficac

Course Structure:

 

Course Introduction

Course Introduction

Welcome to the ASI Learning Organization Course!

This hybrid course consists of over 20 lessons of varying lengths and should be taken in the order they appear in the modules.

Online course details

Each online module may contain the following:

  • Lesson recaps are at the beginning of each module to review the previous learning.
  • Module materials comprise suggested readings, videos, and resources to learn about the covered topics.
  • Knowledge checks are at the end of the lesson, which include learning journal prompts and a reflection link for sharing comments on the lesson.

Live practice details

These sessions allow you to practice what you have learned during the online portion of this course. It is an excellent opportunity to connect with and learn from the ASI team.

  • Sign up and check the calendar for details.
  • Make sure you submit all the required assignments before signing up for a live practice session.

Course Experience

To get the most out of these sessions, dedicate uninterrupted time to learn and reflect. Spend time with your knowledge checks, reflecting on the course material, and applying the concepts. Practice what you have learned and sign up for the live practice sessions.

Enjoy your learning through this self-paced schedule, and let us know if you have any questions.

Happy Learning!

The Learning & Development Team
